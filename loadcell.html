Loadcell

A load cell is a transducer that is used to convert mechanical force or weight into an electrical signal. It is a device that measures the amount of force or weight applied to it and converts this into an electrical signal that can be measured and recorded. Load cells are commonly used in a wide range of applications, including industrial weighing systems, laboratory testing equipment, and aerospace applications.

Load cells can be used in a variety of configurations, including compression, tension, and shear. They can also be designed to measure different ranges of force, from a few grams to many tons. Load cells are often used in combination with other devices, such as amplifiers and displays, to provide accurate and reliable measurements of force and weight.
